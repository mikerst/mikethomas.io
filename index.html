Hi there, I'm Mike, a multiple-hat-wearing designer living and working in London. I'm in the process of reimagining my personal site however in the mean time you can learn a bit more about me below. If you have any further questions, or just want to get in touch, grab me on twitter, or send me an email.

I'm currently a Senior Designer at TotallyMoney.com, working on a variety of different projects across the business, ranging from UI design for new products, through to completely overhauling our email design and development workflow.

I have a broad range of high-end skills, and can slot in mostly anywhere within the product design cycle, whether that be distilling project requirements in to coherent and useful wireframes, mocking up high-fidility visuals, prototyping animations or writing big-project-friendly LESS/CSS.

I've worked professionally as a designer in various forms since 2005. I've been self-employed, agency side and in-house (as well as a mixture of both) and have been lucky enough to have worked with some big-ticket clients over the years. I'm as good at getting my hands dirty on the nuts and bolts of a project as I am at building, organising and motivating a team.

I've worked with a lot of organisations, big and small. I come replete with opinions, here are a few of them...

  • The front-endification of design is a good thing. But with great power, comes great responsibility! Pick the right tool for the job.
  • No processes > too many processes.
  • Process and Method are not the same thing (good recurring processes help facilitate a good methodology).
  • Common sense and intuition are just as valuable in making decisions as hard data. Gut instinct shouldn't be ignored.
  • Your users should always come first, but they are not always right.
  • Better Call Saul is a better show than Breaking Bad. Yep.
  • It is ok to say you're 'going to the bathroom' even if no bath is present in that room.
